Step 1 — Link Your Discord Account

Linking your Discord unlocks your JMBN profile, mission attendance, CI tracking, and payouts.

How the link works

Follow these steps:

  • 1. On the JMBN website, click “Link Discord”.
  • 2. You’ll receive a unique link key (a short code).
  • 3. Go to the JMBN Discord server.
  • 4. Use the link command with your key:
    /link <your-key-here>
  • 5. If successful, the bot will confirm and your account will be linked.

What gets linked?

  • Your Discord ID ↔ JMBN profile record.
  • Your in-game handle / callsign.
  • Your rank and certifications.
  • Mission attendance and CI history.

Once linked, you’ll be able to use profile, mission, and certification commands directly from Discord.

Tip: Keep your link key private. If you ever feel it was exposed, ask staff to invalidate and regenerate one.

Step 2 — Your Profile & Rank Progression

Your profile is your identity inside JMBN — it tracks who you are, what you fly, and where you stand.

Profile Overview

You can view your profile in Discord anytime:

  • /profile — View your own profile.
  • /profile view @member — View someone else’s profile (if allowed).

A profile usually includes:

  • • Callsign / handle
  • • Primary roles (Hauling / Escort / VBSS / Medical / etc.)
  • • Current rank (Enlisted, Warrant, Officer)
  • • Contribution Index (CI)
  • • Certifications & key ships

Ranks & Expectations

JMBN rank is based on trust, performance, and consistency — not favoritism.

  • Enlisted: New crew, learning the SOPs, showing up to missions.
  • Warrant: Proven operators who can take ownership of tasks.
  • Officer: Mission leaders, planners, and organizers.

If you perform and you’re reliable, your path upwards is open. We don’t gatekeep — we support.

Tip: Keep your roles updated so planners know where to place you in multi-crew setups.

Step 3 — Missions, Attendance & Payouts

Missions are the heart of JMBN. The Discord bot ties missions, attendance, and CI together so we can pay everyone fairly.

Joining Missions

Typical flow:

  • 1. A mission is created via /mission create (by staff or a trusted planner).
  • 2. The bot posts an embed with mission details and buttons.
  • 3. Click I’m Going to join the roster.
  • 4. If plans change, click Withdraw or Not Going.

Attendance is automatically logged and synced to the Mission Log & CI system.

Viewing Missions

  • /mission list — View upcoming / active missions.
  • /mission view <id> — View a single mission’s details.

Missions typically include:

  • • Title & mission type (Hauling, Patrol, Escort, VBSS)
  • • Date/time & duration
  • • Planned payout and split
  • • Ship / role requirements

Payouts & Your Share

For missions that generate org income, the result is logged and assigned to participants. Over the month:

  • • Every attended mission contributes to your CI.
  • • The org sets aside a payout pool.
  • • Your CI determines the size of your slice.

In short: The more you show up and contribute, the more you earn.

Step 4 — Certifications & Contribution Index (CI)

Certifications show what you’re trained for. CI shows how much you’ve been contributing over time.

Certifications

Examples (these are customizable to your org):

  • • VBSS Qualified
  • • Capital Ship Bridge Crew
  • • Escort Wing Pilot
  • • Logistics / Cargo Lead
  • • Medical Support

You can view your certs via:

  • /certs — View your certifications.
  • /certs view @member — View another member (if allowed).

Contribution Index (CI)

CI is a value that increases as you contribute:

  • • Attending missions
  • • Taking up roles in ops
  • • Supporting with prep, logistics, and org activities

CI is used when the org distributes monthly payouts, so people who consistently help get rewarded fairly.

Tip: If you’re not sure what to go for first, start with a basic Ops / Hauling role, then work towards VBSS or Capital Ship certs.

FAQ & Troubleshooting

Common issues and how to solve them quickly.

The bot says my link key is invalid.
Make sure you copied the entire key (no spaces cut off). Keys usually expire after a short time. If it still fails, request a new key from the website or ask staff to reset your link.
I can’t see any slash commands.
Ensure you’re in the correct Discord server and that the bot is visible. If you still can’t see commands, check if you’ve disabled “Use slash commands” in your client, or re-sync the app by closing and reopening Discord.
I joined a mission but I don’t see it in my records.
Make sure you clicked I’m Going on the mission message before the mission starts. If the mission was updated or remade, check the latest mission post. For anything missing, raise it with staff so they can manually verify and correct attendance.
The bot isn’t responding at all.
First, check if it responds to /help or another simple command in the correct channel. If there’s still no response, the bot may be restarting or offline. Ping an officer or support channel so they can check the status.
I want to change my callsign / main role.
Use the profile edit command if available, or contact staff to update it in the system. Keeping this accurate helps planners assign ships and roles correctly.
Still stuck? Take a screenshot, note which command you used, and post it in the support / help channel so we can troubleshoot quickly.

Discord Command Summary

A quick reference for commonly used JMBN bot commands. Actual commands may vary slightly based on your implementation.

Command Usage Description
/link <key> Example: /link ABC123-XYZ Links your Discord account to your JMBN profile using the key from the website.
/profile - Shows your profile — rank, CI, roles, and basic info.
/profile view @user @member View another member’s profile (if permission allows).
/mission list - Lists active / upcoming missions you can join.
/mission view <id> Mission ID Shows full mission details, including time, type, payout, and attendees.
/mission create Fields / options Create a new mission (usually restricted to staff / planners).
/certs - View your certifications and qualified roles.
/certs view @user @member View another member’s certifications (if allowed).
/help - Shows a help summary with available commands and categories.
Note: You can customize these commands in your bot back-end. This page is meant to be a friendly onboarding reference, not strict documentation.
