1. Lesson Aim & Fire Team Structure
FTM–1
Lesson Aim
On completion of this lesson, the recruit is expected to:
  • •State the purpose of a Fire Team.
  • •Identify all roles in a 4-man Fire Team.
  • •Describe who controls formation, pace, and reactions.
Training context:
  • •Ship interior and bunker operations.
  • •Surface movement from ship to objective.
  • •Transition between open terrain and confined spaces.
Standard Fire Team Composition (4-Man)
  • •Team Leader (TL) – Commands formation, direction, speed, and reactions. Responsible for overall control.
  • •Point / Rifleman 1 (F1) – First in contact, clears corners, leads entries under TL direction.
  • •Rifleman 2 (F2) – Supports point, maintains formation integrity, assists with angles and flanks.
  • •Support / AR (F3) – Provides suppression, anchors the team when static, reinforces weak sectors.
  • •Rear Security (F4) – Responsible for 6 o’clock. Prevents surprise attacks from behind.
Standard rule:
  • •Roles are assigned before departure or drop.
  • •Role changes are only carried out on TL’s explicit orders.
  • •Each member must know their role and sector before any movement begins.
2. Standard Fire Team Formations
FTM–2
General

The Fire Team adjusts its formation according to terrain and expected threat. The TL is responsible for selecting and announcing the formation prior to movement and when changing formation.

Wedge Formation
Standard – Open Ground
F1 F2 TL F3 F4

Used in open terrain and on approaches where visibility is good. Provides effective 360° observation and flexible reaction left or right.

Use: Open ground Advancing to objective
File Formation
Narrow Spaces
F1 TL F2 F3 F4

Used in corridors, ship passageways, stairwells and tight bunkers. Keeps the team compact and controlled in restricted space.

Use: Ships & corridors Single-door entries
Staggered Column
Roads / Paths
F1 F2 TL F3 F4

Used when moving along roads, paths or channels where threat is expected from both flanks. Provides balanced coverage left and right.

Use: Tracks / roads Balanced flank cover
Echelon (Left / Right)
Threat One Side
Echelon Right: F1 TL F2 F3 F4

Used when the team is moving along a wall, cliff or other linear feature and threat is assessed primarily from one side.

Use: Wall / cliff One-sided threat
Line Formation
Assault / Breach
F1 TL F2 F3 F4

Used when maximum firepower to the front is required, such as before a breach, across open doors, or when sweeping a hangar.

Use: Breach line Hangar sweep
3. Movement Techniques
FTM–3
Bounding Overwatch

Bounding overwatch is used when enemy contact is possible or likely. One element moves while the other remains stationary and provides security.

Procedure:

  • 1.Element A halts and takes up firing positions. Element A calls: “A set, covering.”
  • 2.Element B calls: “B moving.” and moves to the next covered position.
  • 3.On arrival, Element B calls: “B set.”
  • 4.Element A then calls: “A moving.” and bounds past B.
  • 5.Sequence repeats until the objective or safe position is reached.

At any time, at least one element must be stationary and ready to fire.

Peeling (Breaking Contact)

Peeling is used to disengage from contact while maintaining fire and control. The team withdraws by rotating one member at a time to the rear.

Procedure:

  • 1.TL orders: “Peel left!” or “Peel right!”
  • 2.Front man fires short controlled bursts, then calls: “Moving!”
  • 3.Front man moves behind the last man in the team and takes up a new firing position.
  • 4.The next man in line repeats the process.
  • 5.Peel continues until team reaches a designated position or is directed to hold.

Peeling must be controlled, with clear calls and no uncontrolled sprinting.

Key Principle

Movement only occurs when another member or element is prepared to cover. Before leaving cover, the moving member confirms that someone is “Set” and watching the threat direction.

4. Sectors & Spacing
FTM–4
Sector Responsibilities

Each member is responsible for a specific “sector” around the team. This prevents gaps in observation and reduces duplication.

  • •F1 (Point): Front-right (approx. 11–1 o’clock depending on formation).
  • •TL: Front and general control of angles.
  • •F2: Threat side (flank), checks doors and cross-lanes.
  • •F3: Opposite flank or rear-left, depending on formation.
  • •F4: Rear (6 o’clock), ensures back is protected.
Example – corridor movement:
  • •Point and TL focus on the corridor ahead.
  • •F2/F3 focus on doors and intersections.
  • •F4 regularly checks rear and reports contacts behind.
Spacing
Open terrain:
  • •Maintain approximately 3–5 meters between members.
  • •Prevents one explosion from disabling the entire team.
  • •Makes the team harder to detect at distance.
Indoors / ships:
  • •Maintain approximately 1–2 meters between members.
  • •Allows members to move, turn and fire without colliding.
  • •Avoid blocking doorways, corners and firing lines.
5. Communication & Control
FTM–5
Standard Voice Procedures

The following standard phrases are to be used during Fire Team movement. Phrases are short, clear and understood by all members.

  • •“Moving” – Called by a member when leaving cover to change position.
  • •“Set” – Called by a member when they have reached position and are ready to fire.
  • •“Covering” – Indicates that a member has eyes and weapon on a specific lane or sector.
  • •“On you, [name]” – Indicates that a member is directly following or supporting that person.
  • •“Contact front/left/right/rear” – Announces contact and the direction.
  • •“Crossing” – Used before passing in front of another member’s weapon line.
Discipline

During movement, communication must support control, not create confusion.

  • •Only the TL gives formation and movement orders.
  • •Members report status (“Moving”, “Set”, “Covering”) when required.
  • •Non-essential chat is minimized during contact and during drills.
  • •Contradictory orders are not given. If unclear, request clarification: “Say again, last order.”
Common Faults
  • ✕Bunching up in doorways or at corners.
  • ✕Failure to provide rear security.
  • ✕Moving without confirming that someone is “Set”.
  • ✕All members focusing on the same direction.
  • ✕Running ahead of the TL and breaking formation.
6. Assessment & Pass Criteria
FTM–6
BMT – Fire Team Movements: Assessment Checklist
  • ✔Identifies all Fire Team roles and responsibilities correctly.
  • ✔Forms Wedge, File, Staggered Column, Echelon (L/R), and Line on TL’s command.
  • ✔Executes bounding overwatch using correct “Moving/Set” procedure.
  • ✔Executes peeling drill on command to break contact in a controlled manner.
  • ✔Maintains appropriate spacing in open ground and in confined spaces.
  • ✔Maintains assigned sectors with minimal reminders.
  • ✔Uses standard voice procedures clearly and consistently.
  • ✔Maintains formation and does not rush ahead of TL without orders.

Successful completion of this lesson contributes towards the BMT – Fire Team Movements component of the BMT certification.

Instructor Notes

Recommended Sequence

  • 1.Explain team roles and sectors.
  • 2.Static practice of each formation.
  • 3.Slow walk-through with commands.
  • 4.Add bounding and peeling drills.
  • 5.Run full scenario with timed evaluation.

Formation Selection

  • •Open ground: Wedge.
  • •Road/path: Staggered Column.
  • •Ships/corridors: File.
  • •Wall/cliff: Echelon (towards threat).
  • •Door/hangar: Line.

Key Emphasis

  • •One element covers while the other moves.
  • •Rear security is always assigned.
  • •Comms must be clear, short and relevant.

Safety / Control

  • •Stop drill if formation breaks completely.
  • •Reset and repeat until actions are consistent.
