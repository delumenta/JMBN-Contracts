Fire Team Structure
JMBN default composition for BMT and operations.
Section 1

Standard 4-Man Fire Team

  • •Team Lead (TL) – Commands the team, decides formation, tempo, and reactions.
  • •Point / Rifleman 1 (F1) – First in contact, scans front and immediate danger areas.
  • •Rifleman 2 (F2) – Flank & support, helps maintain formation integrity.
  • •Support / AR (F3) – Suppressive fire, anchors the team during contact.
  • •Rear Security (F4) – Covers 6 o’clock, protects team from back-stab or chase.

In bunkers and ships, this structure keeps 360° security even in tight spaces. The roles do not change mid-run unless ordered by the Team Lead.

Movement Techniques
How the fire team moves under different threat levels.
Section 3

Bounding Overwatch (Leapfrogging)

One element moves while the other covers. This is used when contact is possible or likely, especially across open ground or between cover.

  • 1.Team A: “Set, covering.” — weapons up, scanning.
  • 2.Team B: “Moving.” — advances to next cover.
  • 3.Team B: “Set.” — now they cover.
  • 4.Team A moves to leapfrog past B.

Star Citizen example: Crossing the open area between rocks and bunker entrance on Daymar — one element always has guns up while the other sprints.

Peeling (Break Contact)

Used when you’re under fire and need to fall back without turning into a rout. Each member moves back one by one while others maintain fire.

  • •Command: “Peel right!” or “Peel left!”
  • •Front man fires, then moves behind the last man.
  • •Next man repeats in sequence.

Example: Inside a derelict, heavy enemy push from front corridor — team peels back to the airlock while maintaining fire pressure.

Center Peel (Front Contact)

When taking direct frontal contact and needing to withdraw rapidly, the center elements fall back through the sides in a controlled pattern.

Key idea: no one runs blindly. Everyone knows who moves first and who covers.

Sectors & Spacing
How every member protects part of the 360° bubble.
Section 4

Sector Responsibilities

Every member owns a slice of the 360°. If everyone watches front, no one watches flanks.

  • •F1 (Point): 12 – 2 o’clock
  • •TL: 10 – 12 o’clock
  • •F2: 2 – 4 o’clock
  • •F3: 8 – 10 o’clock
  • •F4 (Rear): 6 o’clock

In a bunker corridor, this means somebody always checks behind the team, even while pushing forward.

Spacing

Outdoors:

  • •3–5 meters apart
  • •Prevents 1 grenade killing entire team
  • •Harder to spot from distance

Indoors / Ships:

  • •1–2 meters apart
  • •Enough space to turn & aim
  • •Don’t block corners or doorways
Communication & Discipline
Simple, clear, no spam on comms.
Section 5

Standard Callouts

  • •“Moving” – when you depart cover.
  • •“Set” – when you’re in position and ready to fire.
  • •“Covering” – when you’re actively watching a lane.
  • •“On you” – when you are following that player.
  • •“Contact front/left/right” – direction of enemy.
  • •“Crossing” – when crossing team mate’s barrel/line.

Keep comms clean. In Star Citizen, desync and chaos already exist – don’t add noise.

Common Mistakes

  • ✕Bunching up – one grenade or spray wipes the team.
  • ✕No rear security – free kill for anyone flanking.
  • ✕Running indoors – TL loses control, corners get rushed.
  • ✕Over-peeking – SC AI & players will delete you.
  • ✕Forgetting sectors – everyone stares same way, nobody checks sides.
Assessment Criteria
What instructors look for during BMT check ride.
Section 6
To Pass Fire Team Movements (BMT)
  • ✔Demonstrate all core formations: Wedge, File, Staggered Column, Echelon, Line.
  • ✔Execute bounding overwatch with clear “Moving / Set” calls.
  • ✔Execute peeling drill under simulated fire.
  • ✔Maintain proper spacing indoors & outdoors.
  • ✔Maintain correct sectors of fire without constant reminders.
  • ✔Follow Team Lead orders quickly with minimal delay.
  • ✔Show discipline on comms – no unnecessary chatter in movement phase.

Quick Reference
Cheat sheet for drills & live ops.
Side Panel

Formation by Environment

  • •Open terrain – Wedge
  • •Roads / valleys – Staggered Column
  • •Ship corridors – File
  • •Caves / tight tunnels – File
  • •Side threat (wall/cliff) – Echelon (towards open side)
  • •Pre-breach / assault – Line

Bounding Drill Script

  • 1.“A set, covering.”
  • 2.“B moving.” – B advances to next cover.
  • 3.“B set.”
  • 4.“A moving.” – A advances past B.

Instructors may ask you to run this repeatedly between rocks, walls, or cover in-game.

Peel Drill Script

  • •TL: “Peel right!”
  • •Front man fires 2–3 shots, shouts “Moving!”, moves behind last man.
  • •Next man repeats.
  • •Continue until team reaches safe position.

Minimum Standard

  • •No bunching up.
  • •No dead sectors (front, flanks, rear always covered).
  • •Clear acknowledgement of commands.
  • •Calm, controlled pace — not rushing.

Recommended Practice

  • •Run drills in a safe moon bunker with AI only.
  • •Practice both day & night conditions.
  • •Swap roles (Point, Rear, TL) to feel each perspective.
