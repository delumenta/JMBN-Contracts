1. Purpose & Fire Team Structure
Ref: FTM–1
Lesson Objective
By the end of this lesson, a recruit should:
  • •Understand the roles in a 4-man Fire Team.
  • •Know who controls pace, formation, and reactions.
  • •Know when roles may (and may not) be swapped.
Scope:
  • •Bunker operations.
  • •Ship interior clearing.
  • •Surface approaches to targets.
Standard 4-Man Fire Team
  • •Team Lead (TL) – Commands formation, speed, and reactions.
  • •Point / Rifleman 1 (F1) – First contact, clears corners, leads entry.
  • •Rifleman 2 (F2) – Flank support, helps TL keep shape.
  • •Support / AR (F3) – Suppression, anchors the team.
  • •Rear Security (F4) – Watches 6 o’clock, prevents backstab.
Doctrine Note:
Roles should be confirmed before insertion. No mid-run role swapping unless ordered by TL. In Star Citizen, confusion kills faster than bullets.
2. Core Fire Team Formations
Ref: FTM–2
Formations Overview

Fire Team formations change with terrain and threat. There is no “one formation fits all” – the TL must select the correct shape.

Wedge Formation
Default – Open Terrain
F1 F2 TL F3 F4

Standard movement outside bunkers, on pads, and across open fields. Good all-round visibility and quick reaction left/right.

360° security Flexible Approach posture
File Formation
Corridors / Ships
F1 TL F2 F3 F4

Used in ships, stairwells, caves, and tight bunker hallways. Keeps footprint small and movement controlled.

Tight spaces Ship clearing
Staggered Column
Roads / Valleys
F1 F2 TL F3 F4

Used along paths, ravines, or roads on moons/planets. Balances left/right security while staying mobile.

Balanced arcs Movement focused
Echelon (Left / Right)
Threat One Side
Echelon Right: F1 TL F2 F3 F4

Used when hugging a wall or cliff and expecting contact from one side. Stack towards the threat direction.

Wall/comms tower One-sided threat
Line Formation
Assault / Breach
F1 TL F2 F3 F4

Used when preparing to breach rooms, hangars, or to deliver maximum fire to the front.

Max forward fire Door / hangar entry
3. Movement Techniques
Ref: FTM–3
Bounding Overwatch

One element moves while the other covers. Use when contact is possible or likely – especially across open gaps between cover.

  • 1.Element A: “Set, covering.” – guns up, scanning.
  • 2.Element B: “Moving.” – moves to next cover.
  • 3.Element B: “Set.” – now they cover.
  • 4.Element A: “Moving.” – leapfrogs past B.

Example: crossing the open pad into a bunker entrance – one element always holds guns while the other sprints.

Peeling (Break Contact)

Used when falling back under fire without turning it into a panicked retreat. The team rotates backwards one by one.

  • •TL: “Peel right!” or “Peel left!”.
  • •Front man fires 2–3 shots, calls “Moving!”, runs behind last man.
  • •Next man repeats until everyone has shifted back.

Example: heavy push in a bunker corridor — peel back in a controlled chain to the previous room or airlock.

Key Principle

No one moves unless someone is covering. Every movement call must have a partner who is already “Set” and ready.

4. Sectors & Spacing
Ref: FTM–4
Sector Responsibilities

Every member owns a slice of the 360° circle:

  • •F1 (Point): 12 – 2 o’clock (front-right)
  • •TL: 10 – 12 o’clock (front-left)
  • •F2: 2 – 4 o’clock (right flank)
  • •F3: 8 – 10 o’clock (left flank)
  • •F4 (Rear): 6 o’clock (rear security)
Corridor note:
TL and point focus forward; F2/F3 watch side rooms and cross-lanes; F4 checks rear regularly. If everyone stares forward, nobody sees the flank push.
Spacing Doctrine
Outdoors:
  • •3–5 meters apart.
  • •Prevents one grenade from wiping the squad.
  • •Harder to spot at distance.
Indoors / Ships:
  • •1–2 meters apart.
  • •Enough room to turn and aim.
  • •Do not block corners or doorways.
5. Communication & Discipline
Ref: FTM–5
Standard Callouts
  • •“Moving” – leaving cover.
  • •“Set” – arrived, ready to fire.
  • •“Covering” – actively watching a lane.
  • •“On you [name]” – following that player.
  • •“Contact front/left/right” – enemy direction.
  • •“Crossing” – when crossing someone’s barrel.

Short, clear phrases only. Star Citizen already has desync and chaos – don’t add extra noise on comms.

Common Mistakes
  • ✕Bunching up – one grenade or spray deletes the team.
  • ✕No rear security – free kills for anyone behind you.
  • ✕Running indoors – TL loses control; corners get rushed.
  • ✕Over-peeking – repeating the same corner until you die.
  • ✕Everyone staring forward – flanks or rear wide open.
6. Assessment & Pass Criteria
Ref: FTM–6
BMT – Fire Team Movements: Pass Conditions
  • ✔Demonstrate Wedge, File, Staggered Column, Echelon, and Line correctly.
  • ✔Execute bounding overwatch using “Moving / Set” correctly.
  • ✔Execute peeling drill under simulated fire.
  • ✔Maintain proper spacing indoors and outdoors.
  • ✔Keep assigned sectors covered with minimal reminders.
  • ✔Follow TL commands quickly and calmly.
  • ✔Maintain comms discipline (no spam in contact).

On successful completion, candidate is eligible for BMT certification component: BMT – Fire Team Movements.
