Fire Team Structure
Who does what inside a 4-man element.
Section 1

Standard 4-Man Fire Team

  • •Team Lead (TL) – Commands formation, speed, and reactions.
  • •Point / Rifleman 1 (F1) – First contact, clears corners and leads entry.
  • •Rifleman 2 (F2) – Flank & support; helps TL maintain formation.
  • •Support / AR (F3) – Suppression, anchors the team in fights.
  • •Rear Security (F4) – Watches the 6; prevents surprise from behind.

In bunkers and ships, roles should be locked before insertion. No mid-run role swapping unless ordered by the TL — chaos kills more teams than enemies.

Movement Techniques
How the team moves under different threat levels.
Section 3

Bounding Overwatch (Leapfrog)

One element moves while the other covers. Use when contact is possible or likely — especially across open gaps between cover.

  • 1.Team A: “Set, covering.” – guns up, scanning.
  • 2.Team B: “Moving.” – moves to next cover.
  • 3.Team B: “Set.” – now they cover.
  • 4.Team A: “Moving.” – leapfrogs past B.

Example: Crossing the open pad into a bunker entrance – one element always holds guns while the other sprints.

Peeling (Break Contact)

Used when you must fall back under fire without turning into a panicked retreat. The team rotates backwards one by one.

  • •TL: “Peel right!” or “Peel left!”
  • •Front man fires 2–3 shots, calls “Moving!”, runs behind last man.
  • •Next man repeats until everyone has shifted back.

Example: Heavy push in a bunker corridor — peel back in a controlled chain to the previous room or airlock.

Center Peel (Front Contact)

When taking direct frontal contact, center elements fall back through the sides in sequence while flanks maintain fire.

Key idea: no one moves without someone covering. Everyone knows who moves first, and where they are going.

Sectors & Spacing
Staying alive by owning a slice of the 360° bubble.
Section 4

Sector Responsibilities

Every member owns a slice of the circle. If everyone stares forward, nobody is watching flanks.

  • •F1 (Point): 12 – 2 o’clock (front-right)
  • •TL: 10 – 12 o’clock (front-left)
  • •F2: 2 – 4 o’clock (right flank)
  • •F3: 8 – 10 o’clock (left flank)
  • •F4 (Rear): 6 o’clock (rear security)

In corridors: TL & point focus front, F2/F3 watch side rooms and cross-lanes, F4 regularly checks rear.

Spacing

Outdoors:

  • •3–5 meters apart.
  • •Prevents one grenade wiping the squad.
  • •Makes team harder to spot at distance.

Indoors / Ships:

  • •1–2 meters apart.
  • •Enough room to turn and aim.
  • •Don’t block corners or doorways.
Communication & Discipline
Simple calls, clean comms, controlled tempo.
Section 5

Standard Callouts

  • •“Moving” – leaving cover.
  • •“Set” – arrived, ready to fire.
  • •“Covering” – actively watching a lane.
  • •“On you” – following the named player.
  • •“Contact front/left/right” – enemy direction.
  • •“Crossing” – when crossing someone’s barrel.

Short, clear phrases only. Star Citizen already has desync and chaos – don’t add extra noise on comms.

Common Mistakes

  • ✕Bunching up – one grenade or spray deletes the team.
  • ✕No rear security – free kills for anyone behind you.
  • ✕Running indoors – TL loses control, corners get rushed.
  • ✕Over-peeking – repeating the same corner until you die.
  • ✕Everyone staring forward – flanks or rear wide open.

Quick Reference
Cheat sheet for drills & live ops.

Formation by Environment

  • •Open terrain – Wedge
  • •Roads / valleys – Staggered Column
  • •Ships / corridors – File
  • •Caves / tight – File
  • •Wall / cliff side – Echelon (toward open side)
  • •Pre-breach – Line

Bounding Drill Script

  • 1.“A set, covering.”
  • 2.“B moving.” – B moves to cover.
  • 3.“B set.”
  • 4.“A moving.” – A leapfrogs.

Peel Drill Script

  • •TL: “Peel right/left!”
  • •Front: fire → “Moving!” → runs to back.
  • •Next repeats until safe.

Minimum Standard

  • •No bunching up.
  • •Front, flanks, and rear covered.
  • •Clear, short comms.
  • •Controlled pace, not rushing.

Recommended Practice

  • •Run drills in a low-risk bunker with AI only.
  • •Practice both day and night conditions.
  • •Rotate TL / Point / Rear roles across runs.
